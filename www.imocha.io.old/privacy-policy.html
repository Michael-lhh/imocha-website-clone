How iMocha Processes Your Personal Information

Data protection laws and privacy laws in certain jurisdictions, like the EuropeanEconomic Area (EEA), differentiate between “controllers” and “processors” of personal information. A controller decides why and how to process personal information.

A processor processes personal information on behalf of a controller based on the controller’s instructions. When iMocha processes your Customer AccountData, the iMocha entity with whom you are contracting is acting as a controller. When we process the information on your behalf (employee and candidate data), you are the controller and iMocha is the processor.

Broadly speaking, we use Customer Account Data to further our legitimate interests to:

  • Understand who our customers and potential customers are and their interests in iMocha’s product and services,
  • Fulfil our contractual obligations to the Customer
  • Manage our relationship with you and other customers,
  • Carry out core business operations such as accounting, filing taxes, and fulfilling regulatory obligations and
  • Help detect, prevent, or investigate security incidents, fraud and other abuse and/or misuse of our products and services.
