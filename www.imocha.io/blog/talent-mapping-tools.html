Top 8 Talent Mapping Tools to Close Skill Gaps in 2026

Table of contents

Text Link

Talent mapping tools help organizations analyze workforce skills, detect gaps, and align talent with evolving business needs. These solutions harness real-time data, predictive insights, and artificial intelligence to design competency frameworks, support succession planning, enable career pathing, and foster continuous talent development.

Talent mapping involves more than filling roles. It is about uncovering employee potential, designing future-ready career trajectories, and building an adaptable workforce aligned to business goals.

In this blog, we explore the top 8 talent mapping tools that organizations are using in 2025 to address and close critical skills gaps.

2. The Predictive Index

The Predictive Index (PI) is a behavioral and cognitive assessment platform designed to help organizations align talent with strategy. It supports data-driven hiring, workforce planning, and leadership development using psychometric assessments.

Key Features

  • Behavioral Assessment: Measures personality traits and workplace behavior in just 6 minutes.
  • Cognitive Assessment: Evaluates learning ability and problem-solving to predict job performance.
  • Talent Mapping: Aligns behavioral profiles with role requirements for strategic workforce planning.
  • Succession Planning: Helps identify internal candidates for future leadership roles.

Pros:

  • Through the combination of psychometrics and technology, P.I. provides you with tools that help diagnose employee experience and identify specific actions to help improve it
  • This talent mapping tool offers assessments in 70+ languages
  • The software offers one of the fastest behavioral assessments just 6 minutes, with impactful insights into a learner’s behavior

Cons:

  • The Predictive Index is one of the most highly priced workforce development assessment tools, and only enterprises with big budgets can afford it
  • The software has a complex and unintuitive U.I., making it much harder to navigate features like a link to the assessments

Clients: DocuSign, Formstack, AutoNation, 47

3. SmartRecruiters

‍

SmartRecruiters is an enterprise hiring platform designed to streamline talent acquisition at scale. It offers end-to-end recruiting capabilities that enable teams to attract, select, and hire candidates efficiently while collaborating across departments.

Key Features

4. SeekOut

SeekOut is an AI-powered talent intelligence platform designed for advanced candidate sourcing and workforce insights. It enables recruiters to discover hard-to-find talent across multiple channels, including GitHub and public profiles, and supports DEI-focused hiring strategies.

Key Features

  • AI Talent Search: Finds top candidates beyond LinkedIn using filters like skills, experience, and location.
  • GitHub Integration: Identifies and evaluates technical talent based on coding activity and contributions.
  • Diversity Hiring Tools: Supports inclusive recruiting with filters for underrepresented talent pools.
  • Workforce Insights: Provides data to analyze internal talent and inform strategic hiring decisions.

Pros:

  • You can target passive applicants who aren't on LinkedIn or other sites using the platform
  • It allows you to discover candidates with basic keyword searches – eliminating the need of expert search techniques
  • It can look for tech positions internally within GitHub
  • This software provides talent mapping services and allows recruiters to upload job descriptions directly on SeekOut’s AI Matching tool. It helps you find relevant candidates based on the descriptions uploaded

Cons:

  • Their search engine is built poorly. It does not suggest names slightly resembling the one entered, so if you make a spelling mistake, it will not offer any related options
  • This talent mapping software does not allow you to directly add the candidate to a campaign from the search page. You need to open another tab in the project and add them

FAQs

1. What is talent mapping?

Talent mapping is the process of identifying and analyzing the skills, potential, and future readiness of an organization's workforce. It involves evaluating current employee capabilities, forecasting future role requirements, and aligning talent development efforts to support succession planning, internal mobility, and business growth.

2. How to create talent mapping?

Talent mapping begins by identifying critical roles and assessing current employee skills through assessments and performance data. Comparing existing capabilities with future needs helps highlight skill gaps, allowing HR teams to segment talent and plan upskilling, reskilling, or succession accordingly.

3. What tools are used in talent mapping?

‍Tools like iMocha, The Predictive Index, and Zensai support different stages of talent mapping, from skills assessment and benchmarking to succession planning and workforce analytics. These platforms help organizations make informed, data-driven talent decisions.
